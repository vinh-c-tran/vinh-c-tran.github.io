This is a lab project where we fabricated a graphene field effect transistor (FET) to measure the resistivity of the graphene channel as a function of applied gate voltage. Graphene's band structure and density of states predicts that the graphene channel's resistivity would exhibit a sharp peak at the Fermi level. Applying a voltage to the gate would induce charge separation via the electric field effect and by sweeping through a range of voltage values the energy level should sweep from below to above the Fermi level allowing us to perhaps see this effect for ourselves. Through this project, we got hands on experience with fabricating this FET via photolithography and automating measurements via equipment interfacing with LabView.
